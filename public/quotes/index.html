Quotes

I like hoarding pearls of wisdom that I come across while reading books, blog posts, HN and papers. Below is a growing collection of such tidbits. If you find any quote lacking or having a misplaced attribution please let me know.

Show me your code and conceal your data structures, and I shall continue to be mystified. Show me your data structures, and I won’t usually need your code; it’ll be obvious. - Eric Raymond

From an educational point of view, our experience suggests that undergraduate computer science courses should emphasize basic notions of modularity, specification, and data abstraction, and should not let these be displaced by more advanced topics, such as design patterns, object-oriented methods, concurrency, functional languages, and so on. - Jackson & Chapin on SICP

It is not a language's weakness but its strengths that control the gradient of its change: Alas, a language never escapes its embryonic sac. - Alan Perlis

A distributed system is one in which the failure of a computer you didn't even know existed can render your own computer unusable. - Leslie Lamport
